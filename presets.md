# Preset Differences — Vault vs Sealed

The theme ships with two presets. Both use the same sections, blocks, and metafields — the difference is entirely in the **visual system** and a handful of behaviour toggles.

## When to use which

| Use **Vault** if… | Use **Sealed** if… |
|---|---|
| You primarily sell singles | You primarily sell sealed product (boxes, ETBs, bundles) |
| Your catalog is 11–100+ SKUs today, growing toward 500+ (Lots) | Your catalog is 11–100+ sealed + supply SKUs (Some) |
| You want a dark, gritty, data-forward aesthetic | You want a lighter, product-hero aesthetic |
| Your product images are cards (2.5×3.5 portrait) | Your product images are sealed boxes / supplies (square) |
| You need fast scanning over dense product grids | You want elevated product cards with room to breathe |

## Switching presets

In the theme editor: **Theme settings → Presets → pick Vault or Sealed**.

Switching:
- **Rewrites:** color schemes, card styles, corner radii, shadows, button styling, cart type, badge position, predictive search settings, vendor display defaults
- **Does NOT change:** sections on each page, block configurations, metafields, navigation, products, collections

You can switch back and forth safely. Customizations you make in each preset are saved per-preset.

| **Button radius** | 0 | 4 |

## Visual philosophy

**Vault** is designed for speed and scanning: readers' eyes go straight to prices, set codes, and condition data. Sharp corners, tight spacing, and high-contrast dark schemes keep the catalog feeling like a tool.

**Sealed** is designed for consideration: larger cards, softer edges, and vendor cues put product packaging and brand in focus. Perfect for shoppers browsing a curated sealed/supplies catalog rather than hunting down a specific card.

## Can I mix and match?

Yes. The presets are starting points — every setting is customizable in **Theme settings**. Common customizations:
- Vault with softer card radii (8px)
- Sealed with the dark scheme-4 swapped into scheme-1
- Custom per-game accent colors overriding the defaults

Make those changes in the theme editor; they persist within the active preset.
